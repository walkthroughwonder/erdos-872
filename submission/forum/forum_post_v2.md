## Body

I tried to attack the residual restricted safe-edge hypothesis (Definition 4.5, used in Theorem 4.7 and Proposition A.9) by proposing a trap-aware Maker strategy in [Buddhdev's slot game](https://www.sensho.xyz/papers/erdos-872.pdf). The first version was wrong; the corrected analysis surfaced what looks like a real local obstruction. The reason I am posting here is that the obstruction may be useful information for others working on the conditional T2 bound.

### 1. Setup, faithfully restated

In Buddhdev's Appendix A.1, a residual state is `(C, D_V, D_E, K, S)` and the scaled potential is `Q = 8 S + sum over live e of Phi(e)`, where `Phi(e) = 2^|e cap C| w(e)`. Proposition A.3 (the finite `M/8` capture bound) requires per-round nondecrease of `Q` from every pre-Maker state generated by the strategy. Definition 4.5 (the restricted safe-edge hypothesis) is exactly that nondecrease condition restricted to the T2-strategy-generated states.

One ambiguity worth flagging: the manuscript's description of Maker's ordinary capture ("adding its vertices to `C` and adding its weight to `S`") does not explicitly say the captured edge is removed from the live set. The proof of Proposition A.3 (Q-monotonicity from `Q_0 = sum w(e)` to `Q_fin = 8 S_fin`) only works if ordinary-captured edges are removed from live (otherwise they would remain with `Phi = 8` and prevent termination). My code therefore treats ordinary capture as also placing `e` into a captured-removed-from-live set, equivalent to adding it to `K`. If anyone reads Appendix A.1 differently, the verification below should be re-checked under that reading.

The natural "fiber" geometry of Proposition A.2's `K_4` counterexample is a single large-prime slot `q` shared by all six rank-3 edges over the small primes `{13, 17, 19, 23}`.

### 2. What I checked computationally

I enumerated multi-fiber rank-3 slot hypergraphs (each fiber a copy of `K_k` over a private `q`) and built a finite safety-game synthesizer that searches for a Maker strategy whose chosen move has nonnegative worst-case full-round `Q` delta against every legal Shortener reply. (This is the correct test relative to Definition 4.5; an earlier version of the code mistakenly checked the existence of *any* safe move at the visited state, not the safety of the chosen move.)

Exhaustive synthesis results (`v4_combined_results.json` in the repo):

```
Buddhdev K_4 q-fiber {13,17,19,23}: 97 pre-Maker states, 0 unsafe-chosen, S_fin = 1 >= M/8.
Configuration [4, 2]:                 790 pre-Maker states, 0 unsafe-chosen, S_fin = 2 >= M/8.
Configuration [4, 3]:               4 514 pre-Maker states, 0 unsafe-chosen, S_fin = 2 >= M/8.
Configuration [3, 3, 3]:            3 269 pre-Maker states, 0 unsafe-chosen, S_fin = 3 >= M/8.
```

So far, a finite synthesized strategy passes Proposition A.2 itself (the famous Buddhdev refutation) and a handful of multi-fiber `K_3`/`K_4` configurations.

### 3. What broke (the negative result)

The same synthesizer proves, by backward safety-game search, that **no purely local per-round Q-monotone Maker strategy exists for the isolated unit-weight `K_5` `q`-fiber**.

The forced sequence:
1. Maker captures `e_{1,2}`. Shortener replies by deleting side vertex `3q`. `Q: 10 -> 38 -> 26`, full-round delta `+16`.
2. Maker captures `e_{4,5}`. Shortener edge-deletes `e_{1,4}`. `Q: 26 -> 48 -> 40`, delta `+14`.
3. The resulting state has three live edges, all with `Phi = 8`, no vertex deletions available that help Maker, and an edge-deletion budget for Shortener. Maker's best move changes `Q` by `0`; Shortener's reply deletes a `Phi = 8` live edge, producing full-round delta `-8`. The safety-game solver finds no strategy that escapes.

In particular, the `[5]` initial state itself is losing for any local Q-monotone strategy. The first counterexample state (reached by the above forced sequence) is:

```
C = {q, 1q, 2q, 4q, 5q}, D_V = {3q}, D_E = {e_{1,4}}, S = 2,
live = {e_{1,5}, e_{2,4}, e_{2,5}}, all Phi = 8, Q = 40.
```

Full reproducer in `src/v4_k5_bad_path.py` of the repo. The result is exhaustive: the synthesizer is a finite safety-game solver over the full reachable state graph and certifies that every local Maker strategy fails on this configuration.

### 4. What this seems to mean for Definition 4.5

The restricted safe-edge hypothesis cannot be salvaged by any rule that examines only the current residual state and chooses a single live edge based on local features such as fiber-local trap geometry. The `K_5` `q`-fiber forces a state where every Maker move loses to a Shortener edge-deletion. For Definition 4.5 to hold on T2-generated states, the T2-strategy-generation process must already exclude the bad `K_5`-style geometries before they form, presumably via the activation phase or the choice of `B_{a, c}`.

Concrete questions for anyone working on this:

  (a) Does the activation strategy in Proposition A.6 actually rule out a `q`-fiber containing five or more small-prime pairs? If so, the worst residual fiber size is `K_4` and the safety synthesizer's positive result on Proposition A.2's exact geometry would matter.
  (b) Alternatively, is there a global compensation across fibers (e.g. weight migration via the activation residual) that lets a high-`Phi` cluster in one fiber be offset by gains in another? The single-fiber `K_5` synthesizer cannot see this.
  (c) Is the residual game in Appendix A actually the right potential function? Buddhdev's Remark 4.6 explicitly flags that the restricted hypothesis is "the narrowest form compatible with the paper's own refutation." The `K_5` result suggests it may still be too broad if isolated `K_k` fibers can appear after activation for `k >= 5`.

### 5. What I am explicitly not claiming

I am not claiming a refinement of Proposition A.9. I am not claiming Theorem 4.7 is unconditional. I am not claiming a proof of any part of Definition 4.5. An earlier draft of this work made overreaching claims; the GitHub repo's `RETRACTION.md` documents the corrections.

What I am offering is: a manuscript-faithful restatement of the slot-game potential, a verified computational handling of Proposition A.2's exact `K_4` geometry, and an exhaustive finite-state proof that the isolated `K_5` `q`-fiber is locally hopeless under any per-round Q-monotone strategy.
