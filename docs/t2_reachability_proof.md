# T2 reachability of an isolated unit K5 q-fiber

## Answer

Outcome: **(C), reduction to a manuscript clarification**.

I cannot honestly prove (A), and I cannot honestly certify (B) from the manuscript as written. The obstruction is not arithmetic legality. The obstruction is that Buddhdev's activation rule does not specify a deterministic safe-edge selector, or a structural invariant of the selected safe edges, strong enough to decide whether the ten pairs of a five-prime clique may all be secured while a common large prime q remains live.

The manuscript therefore leaves the relevant reachability notion under-specified. If Buddhdev intends an existentially chosen activation strategy that is allowed to avoid bad residual fibers, the manuscript needs to state the selector or prove a K5-free invariant for every q-fiber generated by that selector. If Buddhdev intends all legal choices of a safe activation edge to be allowed, then the present evidence points toward reachability being plausible, since the arithmetic K5 fiber is easy to realize and the local rank-2 K5 activation subgame admits the ten clique activations as safe moves. The missing step is a full-graph proof that those ten choices are safe in the actual activation graph at the relevant states.

## Manuscript rule extraction

1. In the residual target family, Buddhdev fixes \(Y=n^\delta\), lets \(P_Y\) be the odd primes at most \(Y\), defines \(B_{a,c}=I_{a,c}\cap P\) for \(I_{a,c}=(n/(2ac), n/(ac)]\), and uses targets \(acb\) with \(a<c\), \(a,c\in P_Y\), \(b\in B_{a,c}\) ([Buddhdev manuscript, p. 36, Appendix A.1](buddhdev_872.pdf)).
2. The residual slot hypergraph has vertices \(b,ab,cb\) and unit edges \(e_{a,c,b}=\{b,ab,cb\}\) ([Buddhdev manuscript, p. 36, Appendix A.1](buddhdev_872.pdf)).
3. The slot-game state is \((C,D_V,D_E,K,S)\), an edge is live if it is not in \(D_E\cup K\) and contains no deleted vertex, Maker may capture a live edge or make an alternate-scoring move in the scored variant, and Breaker may delete an uncaptured vertex, delete a live edge, or make a scored-edge reply in the scored variant ([Buddhdev manuscript, pp. 36 to 37, Appendix A.1](buddhdev_872.pdf)).
4. Definition 4.5 restricts the safe-edge hypothesis to states actually reached by the T2 activation strategy and subsequent residual slot construction ([Buddhdev manuscript, p. 11, Definition 4.5](buddhdev_872.pdf)).
5. Proposition A.2 explicitly distinguishes reachability under some legal play from reachability under the specific Prolonger strategies of Proposition A.9 ([Buddhdev manuscript, p. 37, Proposition A.2](buddhdev_872.pdf)). This distinction is exactly the issue here.
6. During activation, each unclaimed pair \(e=(a,c)\) carries a current token set \(B_e(t)\), and Prolonger's rule is: among positive current token-weight pair edges, choose a safe edge supplied by the restricted safe-edge hypothesis, play one live target \(acb\), claim the pair edge, capture \(a,c\), and leave the remaining tokens on that secured pair for the residual phase ([Buddhdev manuscript, p. 40, Proposition A.6](buddhdev_872.pdf)).
7. Proposition A.6 does not define a tie-breaker or canonical selector among safe edges, and it does not state a K5-free, bounded-rank, bounded-degree, or sparsity invariant for the residual q-fibers ([Buddhdev manuscript, pp. 40 to 41, Proposition A.6](buddhdev_872.pdf)).
8. Proposition A.9 then restricts to residual live targets over secured pairs and applies the residual rank-three slot hypergraph to those targets ([Buddhdev manuscript, pp. 42 to 43, Proposition A.9](buddhdev_872.pdf)).

## What would make a K5 q-fiber after activation

Fix five small primes \(p_1,\dots,p_5\) and a large prime \(q\). A residual K5 q-fiber exists at the start of the residual slot game if all ten pairs \((p_i,p_j)\) are secured, the token \(q\) remains live for each of those secured pairs, and no other secured pair retaining token \(q\) is included in the residual subfamily. The ten residual targets are

\[
  p_i p_j q \qquad 1\le i<j\le 5,
\]

and their slot edges are

\[
  \{q,p_iq,p_jq\}.
\]

At the fresh residual slot state used by Proposition A.3 after Proposition A.9 restricts to residual live targets, these ten unit edges would have \(C=\varnothing\), \(S=0\), and \(\Phi=1\) ([Buddhdev manuscript, pp. 36 to 37, Appendix A.1](buddhdev_872.pdf), [Buddhdev manuscript, pp. 42 to 43, Proposition A.9](buddhdev_872.pdf)). This is exactly the local K5 state used in the v4 obstruction.

The arithmetic condition for a common \(q\) is

\[
  q\in \bigcap_{i<j} B_{p_i,p_j},
\]

which is nonempty whenever the largest pair product is less than twice the smallest pair product. Consecutive fixed primes around 100 already satisfy this condition.

## Concrete arithmetic witness for the residual incidence

The script `t2_reachability_code/construct_k5_arithmetic_witness.py` constructs a concrete instance with \(n=10^{20}\), \(\delta=1/8\), \(Y=316\), and

\[
  \{p_1,\dots,p_5\}=\{101,103,107,109,113\}.
\]

The common-prime interval is

\[
  (4806305873305778, 8118860112040269],
\]

and the script finds

\[
  q=4806305873305829.
\]

For each of the ten pairs, it also chooses a distinct securing prime \(b_0(a,c)\in B_{a,c}\), with \(b_0(a,c)\ne q\), and verifies that both \(acb_0(a,c)\) and \(acq\) lie in \((n/2,n]\). It then verifies a legal partial actual play in which Prolonger plays those ten securing targets and Shortener replies with harmless prime singletons larger than \(n/2\). The independent verifier `t2_reachability_code/verify_k5_witness_independent.py` checks the same play by direct divisibility comparisons and confirms that all ten \(acq\) targets survive and form the K5 slot incidence.

This proves the following conditional statement.

**Conditional incidence lemma.** If the activation selector is allowed to secure the ten pairs among \(101,103,107,109,113\), avoid consuming or deleting the common token \(q=4806305873305829\) on those ten pairs, and complete activation without adding any other secured residual pair that retains token \(q\), then the residual construction contains a fresh isolated unit K5 q-fiber.

This is not yet outcome (B), because the remaining manuscript-dependent requirements are that each of those ten pair choices be a safe edge in the full activation graph state in which it is chosen and that the later completion of activation not attach additional residual q-edges to the component.

## Why this is a genuine manuscript ambiguity

The activation sentence in Proposition A.6 is existential: Prolonger chooses "a safe edge supplied by that hypothesis" ([Buddhdev manuscript, p. 40, Proposition A.6](buddhdev_872.pdf)). The manuscript does not specify whether reachability in Definition 4.5 is with respect to:

1. a fixed but unstated canonical safe-edge selector,
2. some selector chosen to make the theorem work,
3. every possible selector that always chooses a safe edge,
4. or every legal activation play consistent with the actual divisibility game.

These readings give different answers to Q1. Under reading 2, Buddhdev might avoid K5 q-fibers by design, but the manuscript needs the design or an invariant. Under reading 3 or 4, an arithmetic K5 fiber is available unless the full activation safe-edge condition rules out securing all ten clique pairs.

The ambiguity is sharpened by Proposition A.2, which states that its K4 counterexample is reachable under some legal play sequence but not necessarily under the specific Prolonger strategies of Proposition A.9 ([Buddhdev manuscript, p. 37, Proposition A.2](buddhdev_872.pdf)). That sentence shows Buddhdev is aware that unrestricted legal reachability and strategy-generated reachability are different notions. The K5 question depends on the same distinction, but the actual selector for strategy-generated activation states is not stated.

## Computational checks performed

1. `construct_k5_arithmetic_witness.py` constructs the concrete arithmetic K5 incidence and verifies interval membership, legal Prolonger targets, harmless Shortener replies, and survival of the ten \(acq\) residual targets.
2. `verify_k5_witness_independent.py` independently verifies the same concrete numbers by direct divisibility and slot-incidence checks.
3. `check_activation_k5_sequence.py` verifies a limited local fact: in an isolated rank-2 activation graph on five vertices with initial token weight 2 on each pair, claiming all ten K5 pairs in lexicographic order is safe at every step for the Proposition A.6 rank-2 potential. This does not certify the full Buddhdev activation graph, because outside pair edges can change safe-edge inequalities.

## Conclusion and requested clarification

The manuscript does not currently contain enough information to decide Q1. A decisive non-reachability proof would need a stated activation selector or an invariant such as: for every large prime \(q\), the graph of secured pairs retaining token \(q\) is K5-free. I found no such lemma or invariant in Section 4.3 or Appendix A.1.

A decisive reachability proof would need to show that the ten K5 pair activations in the concrete witness are safe edges in the full activation graph states generated by Buddhdev's activation rule, and that activation can be completed without attaching additional residual q-edges. I did not prove this full-graph safe-edge and completion certificate.

Therefore the rigorous answer is (C): Q1 reduces to a genuine manuscript clarification about the activation safe-edge selector and any intended q-fiber sparsity invariant.
