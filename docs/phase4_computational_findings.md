# Phase 4: computational verification of RSE on K_k fibers

## Date: 22 May 2026 (afternoon)

## TL;DR

Exhaustive enumeration of all reachable pre-Maker states in the single-fiber
slot game (one large prime `b`, `k` small primes producing the rank-3
hypergraph K_k of edges `e_{i,j} = {b, p_i b, p_j b}`) gives the following.

For `k = 3` (triangle, 3 edges, 4 vertices):
- 28 non-terminal reachable pre-Maker states.
- Raw RSE holds at all 28 (100 percent).
- Certified-RSE (the dominant-capture certificate from GPT-5.5 RSE attempt) also holds at all 28.

For `k = 4` (the K_4 case, 6 edges, 5 vertices, where Proposition A.2 lives):
- 1819 non-terminal reachable pre-Maker states (under arbitrary play).
- Raw RSE FAILS at 216 states (11.87 percent).
- All 216 violations have the same structural signature:
  - 0 vertex deletions in the entire history.
  - Exactly 2 edges captured by ordinary Maker capture.
  - Exactly 2 live edges remain.
  - 144 states with |C| = 4 and live-edge potentials (4, 8).
  - 72 states with |C| = 5 (full hub) and live-edge potentials (8, 8).
- Under any of three concrete Maker strategies (max-gain, dominant-capture,
  one-step lookahead), Breaker can force the game into a violation state in
  exactly 2 rounds. The play sequence is identical for all three strategies:

```
Round 0 (initial): C = empty, all 6 edges live with phi = 1, Q = 6.
  Maker captures e_{1,2} = {b, p_1 b, p_2 b}. (Any equivalent capture works.)
  Breaker deletes the unscored live edge e_{1,3} = {b, p_1 b, p_3 b}.
Round 1: C = {b, p_1 b, p_2 b}, D_E = {e_{1,3}}, S = 1.
  Maker captures e_{3,4} = {b, p_3 b, p_4 b}.
  Breaker deletes the unscored live edge e_{1,4} = {b, p_1 b, p_4 b}.
Round 2: C = {b, p_1 b, p_2 b, p_3 b, p_4 b}, D_E = {e_{1,3}, e_{1,4}},
         S = 2, only e_{2,3} and e_{2,4} live, both with phi = 8.
  Maker capture or score either live edge yields gain 0.
  Breaker deletes the other live edge for a loss of 8.
  Q decreases by 8 over this final round.
```

This is precisely the configuration used in Buddhdev's Proposition A.2
refutation, except reached by a DIFFERENT sequence of Maker captures combined
with Breaker edge-deletions (not by Maker alternate-scoring of outside edges).

## What this implies

1. The single-fiber model is too coarse to verify RSE: even with no other
   fibers present, Breaker can drive a 4-prime fiber to a Maker-disaster
   state in 2 rounds under any local Maker strategy.

2. Consequently, raw RSE on T2-generated states cannot hold for any
   purely-local Maker strategy. To satisfy RSE, Maker must use information
   beyond the single fiber. The natural candidates are:
   - INTER-FIBER COMPENSATION: Maker can absorb a Q-loss in one fiber as long
     as a parallel positive gain in another fiber dominates it. The total Q
     is conserved (or rises) across the whole game; what fails is the
     PER-ROUND monotonicity, not the GLOBAL monotonicity.
   - FIBER POSTPONEMENT: T2 activation is supposed to control the order in
     which small primes get activated. The 4-prime fiber containing
     {13, 17, 19, 23, q} only becomes problematic if all four small primes
     have been activated AND q is still uncaptured AND no vertex deletions
     have hit the fiber yet. Phase 1 of T2 may already have committed enough
     of q's slot vertices to other edges so that the K_4 trap is unreachable.

3. The dominant-capture certificate (introduced by GPT-5.5 as a checkable
   sufficient condition) is NOT strictly stronger than raw RSE in violation
   counts: on this fiber, Certified-RSE-violations and raw-RSE-violations
   coincide exactly. Whenever raw RSE fails, no edge satisfies the
   dominant-capture inequality either.

## Sharper open question

Buddhdev's Theorem 4.7 needs the restricted hypothesis on STATES GENERATED BY
THE T2 ACTIVATION + RESIDUAL STRATEGY, not on the full reachable state space
of one isolated fiber. Two refinements remain to be checked.

(I) Inter-fiber compensation. Define the multi-fiber state and verify that,
under T2 activation, any per-round Q-decrease in one fiber is dominated by a
guaranteed Q-increase elsewhere. This requires tracking the GLOBAL Q rather
than the per-fiber Q.

(II) Order of activation in T2. If T2 commits small primes in a fixed order
3, 5, 7, ..., the residual fiber over a fixed large prime q does NOT see all
of {3, 5, 7, ...} simultaneously. By the time the four-prime trap could form,
many slot vertices have already been deleted by earlier securing targets, so
the trap state is unreachable. We have not yet enumerated states under this
restriction.

proposes, not a coarser local approximation. The next session should encode
