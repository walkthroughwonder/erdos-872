# Phase 5: multi-fiber and rank-2 activation RSE verification

## Date: 23 May 2026 (early morning)

## TL;DR (one paragraph)

Computational verification across three regimes gives a sharp picture of where
Buddhdev's restricted safe-edge hypothesis (RSE) holds, where it fails, and
which Maker strategies remediate the failures.

  (1) ACTIVATION STAGE (rank-2 graph game, Proposition A.6): RSE holds at
      100 percent on every reachable state tested across k in {3, 4, 5}
      small primes and across six weight patterns (uniform 1/2/3, Mertens-
      decay, lopsided, two-heavy). This is strong evidence that the rank-2
      activation hypothesis is true. Buddhdev's "activation graph states"
      restricted hypothesis is computationally consistent at small scale.

  (2) RESIDUAL STAGE, SINGLE ISOLATED K_4 FIBER (rank-3 hypergraph,
      Proposition A.9): RSE FAILS at 216 of 1819 reachable pre-Maker states
      (11.87 percent) under any local Maker strategy. The K_4-fiber refutation
      pattern of Proposition A.2 is forceable in two rounds.

  (3) RESIDUAL STAGE, MULTI-FIBER (two fibers, multiple sizes): under naive
      max-gain or dominant-capture strategies, K_4 traps still occur at 53
      percent of reachable states in [4,4]. But under a TRAP-AWARE Maker
      strategy that explicitly switches away from near-trap fibers,
      isolated-K_k-trap signatures are eliminated (0 matches in all
      configurations [4,2], [4,3], [4,4]), and overall RSE failures drop by
      80 to 90 percent: [4,2] from 74 to 0, [4,3] from 686 to 48, [4,4] from
      30808 to 5710. The residual 48 failures in [4,3] arise from a
      structurally distinct end-game pattern (vertex-deletion + Phi=8) and
      suggest the trap-aware strategy needs further refinement to capture
      late-game Phi=8 emergent edges.

## What this means for Buddhdev's restricted hypothesis

The hypothesis (Definition 4.5) requires RSE on states generated by the
SPECIFIC T2 activation and residual strategies in Propositions A.6 and A.9.

For ACTIVATION: every test we ran passes 100 percent, including non-uniform
weights and small k. This is the strongest computational evidence we have
that the activation half of the restricted hypothesis is correct.

For RESIDUAL: the hypothesis as stated in Proposition A.3 fails on naive
max-gain Maker. To repair it, the residual strategy must include a TRAP
AVOIDANCE rule. We implemented two such rules:

  trap_aware_score: when a fiber is in a near-trap state, Maker plays
    alternate-scoring rather than ordinary capture. Score does not add new
    vertices to C, so the "all 5 slots in C" Phi=8 configuration is
    postponed.

  trap_aware_switch: when a fiber is in a near-trap state and another fiber
    has positive-weight live edges, Maker plays in the other fiber.

Both eliminate the isolated K_k trap signature completely. Under either
strategy, in configurations [4,2] RSE holds at 100 percent on all reachable
states.

## The residual 48 failures in [4,3]

These all share the same structure: fiber 0 (K_4) has 2 captures, 1 vertex
deletion already (n_dv=1, so the trap detector rules it out), and 1 live
edge of Phi=8. Fiber 1 (K_3) has 1 capture and 1 live edge of Phi=4. The
fail pattern is: Maker plays something in fiber 0 or fiber 1, Breaker edge-
deletes the Phi=8 edge for a loss of 8, which exceeds Maker's gain of 4.

This is NOT the original K_4 trap. It is a structurally distinct end-game
pattern. The strategy needs to detect "any state where a fiber has a Phi=8
live edge AND no other gain in the system exceeds 8". Refining the strategy
to handle this should reduce failures further, but the cleanup is fiber-
local, not asymptotic.

## Key structural insight

The K_4 trap is essentially: 0 vertex deletions plus 2 ordinary captures
yields a state where the only remaining live edges have all-slots-captured,
forcing Phi=8 and forcing Breaker edge-delete to be net negative.

The fix at the strategy level is: NEVER let a fiber reach 2 ordinary captures
without an intervening vertex deletion or scored move. Buddhdev's manuscript
does not specify a strategy this granular in Proposition A.9. The natural
upgrade is to add the rule "in any fiber with 0 vertex deletions and 1 capture
already, the next Maker move in that fiber must be a SCORE (not capture)"
unless a different fiber offers a positive-gain capture.

If this strategy upgrade is incorporated into A.9, then RSE holds on the
strategy-generated states (computational evidence: 100 percent on [4,2],
about 98.4 percent on [4,3], about 90 percent on [4,4]; failures concentrate
on a small structurally distinct end-game pattern that further refinement
should handle).
