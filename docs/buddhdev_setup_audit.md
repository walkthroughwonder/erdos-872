# Buddhdev setup audit for Erdős #872 v4 work

Ground truth from Buddhdev, Section 4.3 and Appendix A.1, especially the paragraph **“Auxiliary slot game and potential”** before Proposition A.1.

## 1. Residual slot hypergraph and state tuple

**Appendix A.1, “Auxiliary slot game and potential” (verbatim from extracted manuscript text).**

> The scored rank-three slot hypergraph \(G(n,\delta)\) has vertex set consisting of the slots \(b, ab, cb\) which occur in the targets above, and has weighted edges
>
> \[
> e_{a,c,b}:=\{b,ab,cb\}, \quad a<c, \quad a,c\in P_Y, \quad b\in B_{a,c},
> \]
>
> each of weight 1. More generally, the finite auxiliary games below are weighted incidence games of rank at most 3. A state has a captured-vertex set \(C\), a deleted-vertex set \(D_V\), an unscored deleted-edge set \(D_E\), a scored edge set \(K\), and total scored weight \(S\). An edge is live if it is not in \(D_E\cup K\) and contains no deleted vertex. Maker may capture a live edge, adding its vertices to \(C\) and adding its weight to \(S\); in the scored hypergraph variant, Maker may instead use an alternate-scoring move to score the target without adding slot vertices to \(C\). Breaker may reply by deleting one uncaptured vertex, by deleting one live edge without scoring it, or in the scored-hypergraph variant by making a scored-edge reply, which adds one live edge to \(K\) and increments \(S\) by its weight.

Thus the manuscript state tuple is

\[
(C,D_V,D_E,K,S).
\]

There is no manuscript field named `captured_edges`. If code tracks Maker-captured edges separately, that is implementation history; mathematically captured/scored edges must be removed from liveness through the scored/claimed edge bookkeeping.

## 2. Potential \(Q\), with score-weight constant 8

**Appendix A.1, “Auxiliary slot game and potential” (verbatim formula).**

> The scaled potential is
>
> \[
> Q := 8S+\sum_{e\ live}2^{|e\cap C|}w(e).
> \]

**Appendix A.1, proof of Proposition A.1 (verbatim notation).**

> For a live edge \(e\), write \(\Phi(e)=2^{|e\cap C|}w(e)\) and \(Q=8S+\sum\Phi(e)\).

For unit-weight rank-three live edges, \(
\Phi(e)\in\{1,2,4,8\}
\), not \(\{0,2,4,8\}\). Deleted vertices affect \(
\Phi
\) only by making the edge non-live.

## 3. Live edge definition and deletions

**Appendix A.1, “Auxiliary slot game and potential” (verbatim).**

> An edge is live if it is not in \(D_E\cup K\) and contains no deleted vertex.

Therefore an edge meeting \(D_V\) is not live. Vertex deletion does not set \(\Phi(e)=0\) as a separate clause; it removes every incident edge from the live sum. An unscored deleted edge is placed in \(D_E\), and a scored edge is placed in \(K\).

## 4. Proposition A.3: exact conditional finite scaled capture statement

**Proposition A.3 (Conditional finite scaled capture), Appendix A.1.**

> Consider a finite weighted incidence game of rank at most 3, equipped with the scaled potential \(Q\) and the Maker–Breaker rules described above. In each full Maker–Breaker round, Breaker may play any one of:
> (i) a vertex deletion: add an uncaptured vertex \(v\in V\setminus C\) to \(D_V\);
> (ii) an unscored edge deletion: add one currently live edge \(e\) to \(D_E\), available in both the graph and scored-hypergraph variants;
> (iii) a scored-edge reply: add one currently live edge \(e\) to \(K\) and increment \(S\) by \(w(e)\), available only in the scored-hypergraph variant.

**Hypothesis required per generated pre-Maker state.**

> Assume the following restricted safe-edge hypothesis: at every pre-Maker state generated by the strategy under consideration with at least one positive-weight live edge, Maker has a legal live edge \(f\) such that every legal Breaker reply from (i)–(iii) applicable to the current variant leaves \(Q\) at least as large as it was before Maker’s move.

**Conclusion.**

> Under this restricted hypothesis, if the game is continued until no positive-weight live edge remains, the final claimed or scored weight is at least one eighth of the initial total edge-weight:
>
> \[
> S_{fin}\ge {1\over 8}\sum_e w(e).
> \]

The proof is monotonicity only: initially \(Q_0=\sum_e w(e)\), finally \(Q_{fin}=8S_{fin}\), and full-round nondecrease gives \(8S_{fin}=Q_{fin}\ge Q_0\).

## 5. Definition 4.5: restricted safe-edge hypothesis

**Definition 4.5 (Restricted safe-edge hypothesis), Section 4.3 (verbatim).**

> In each finite graph or scored rank-three hypergraph state actually reached by the T2 activation strategy and subsequent residual slot construction, assume that whenever a positive-weight live edge exists, Maker has a legal live edge \(f\) such that, after Maker plays \(f\), every legal Breaker reply leaves the scaled potential \(Q\), as defined in the finite slot-game setup of Appendix A, at least as large as it was before Maker’s move.

**Remark 4.6 (context).**

> The narrowing to strategy-generated states is specifically engineered to avoid the counterexample of Proposition A.2: the restricted hypothesis is therefore the narrowest form compatible with the paper’s own refutation of the general safe-edge property, and its plausibility is an open target.

## 6. Proposition A.9: conditional T2 assembly

**Proposition A.9 (Conditional T2 lower bound), Appendix A.1 (verbatim statement).**

> Assume the restricted safe-edge hypothesis of Proposition A.3 for the activation graph states and for the residual slot-hypergraph states generated by the construction above. For every fixed \(0<\delta<1/4\) there is \(c_\delta>0\) such that
>
> \[
> L(n)\ge c_\delta {n(\log\log n)^2\over \log n}
> \]
>
> for all sufficiently large \(n\).

**Key proof sentence.**

> Under the residual restricted safe-edge hypothesis, Proposition A.3 gives a strategy that forces scored residual target weight at least \(M/8\) in this hypergraph model.

This proposition is conditional on restricted safe-edge for both activation graph states and residual slot-hypergraph states. It does not specify v3/v4 as a concrete residual policy.

## 7. Lemma 4.3 for vertex-deletion comparison

**Lemma 4.3 (Maximum-degree right capture), Section 4.2 (verbatim statement).**

> Let \(G=(L\sqcup R,E)\) be a finite bipartite graph. Consider the following finite capture game on the live graph. Maker moves first. On each Maker move, Maker chooses a right vertex of maximum positive live degree, captures all currently live edges incident to it, and removes that right vertex and its incident live edges. On each Breaker move, Breaker may delete one right vertex and all live edges incident to it, delete one live edge, or do nothing. The game stops when no live edge remains.
>
> Let \(C\) be the number of edges captured by Maker, and let \(X\) be the number of edges individually deleted by Breaker. Then, regardless of Breaker’s play,
>
> \[
> C+X\ge |E|/2.
> \]

This is a bipartite fan lemma. It is not a rank-three residual slot-game safe-edge theorem, and it does not by itself bound arbitrary post-Maker deletion of a high-degree slot vertex in \(G(n,\delta)\).
